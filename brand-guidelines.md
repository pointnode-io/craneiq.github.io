## 1. Brand Overview

**craneIQ** is an overhead crane technology platform — a suite of products that sits alongside existing crane controls to add intelligence, safety and monitoring. It is engineered in the West Midlands, UK and installed worldwide.

**Core brand positioning:**
> "Complete crane intelligence — on any crane, from any manufacturer."

**Key brand truths:**
- Works alongside existing controls — nothing replaced, zero risk
- No proprietary lock-in — open architecture, open data
- Commissions in a single shift
- Proven in galvanizing plants, steel mills, foundries and automotive facilities
- UK engineered, globally installed

## 4. Typography

### Typefaces

| Role | Font | Weight | Use |
|------|------|--------|-----|
| Primary | Open Sans | 300 (Light) | `crane` in wordmark, body copy |
| Primary | Open Sans | 400 (Regular) | Body copy, UI labels |
| Primary | Open Sans | 600 (SemiBold) | `IQ` in wordmark, headings, CTAs |
| Primary | Open Sans | 700 (Bold) | Display headings (brochure/print) |
| Mono | Share Tech Mono | 400 | Section labels, product codes, data readouts, technical tags |

### Usage Rules

**Section labels** — Share Tech Mono, 9–10px, letter-spacing 0.2em, uppercase, Brand Teal, with a short horizontal rule prefix (`— LABEL`)

**Product codes** — Share Tech Mono, uppercase. Format: `CIQ-XXX` (e.g. CIQ-COR, CIQ-MON)

**Body copy** — Open Sans Regular, 13–14px on web, 7–8pt in print, line-height 1.7–1.85

**Never use:** Arial, Roboto, Inter, or system fonts in branded materials

## 7. Technical Messaging Rules

**DWP (Design Working Period):**
- Always explained as "counts down from 100% based on actual usage"
- Three components tracked separately: Hoist DWP, Brake DWP, Controls DWP
- Never say "counts up" — it counts down
- Odometer analogy is approved: use it for Monitoring copy

**Protocols — always in this order:**
1. Hardwired I/O (always first — most customers start here)
2. Modbus TCP
3. Profinet
4. EtherNet/IP
5. CANopen (optional, lower priority)

**Antisway:**
- Open-loop algorithm — no sensors, no encoders, no cameras
- Uses existing travel VFDs only
- Single, dual or triple axis
- Never claim specific sway reduction percentages without data

**Vision:**
- Camera on crane structure (not on hook)
- Passive reflective target on hook block — no electronics on the hook
- Works for any reeving arrangement

**Overload:**
- Stops at 110% of rated capacity — do not use as a selling point (this is standard practice)

## 8. Tone of Voice

**Technical but not impenetrable.** Write as a knowledgeable engineer talking to another engineer — not a salesperson.

- Lead with what it does, not what it is
- Be specific — "counts down from 100%" not "tracks wear"
- Avoid superlatives — "dramatically reduces" is fine, "the best" is not
- Never mention competitors by name
- "Worldwide" not "UK/Ireland/EU" — craneIQ is installed globally
- Use "galvanizing" with z (not s) throughout

**Avoid:**
- "Revolutionary", "industry-leading", "cutting-edge", "next-generation"
- Vague safety claims without specifics
- Overclaiming — if it's standard practice, don't present it as a differentiator

## 9. How It Works — Diagram

The canonical signal path diagram is:

```
[Operator Pendant] → [craneIQ Platform] → [Existing Controller] → [Motors & VFDs]
```

- The craneIQ node is labelled **"Platform"** — not "Core" (Core is one product within the platform)
- Cloud is **not** a node in the signal path — it sits within the platform
- The existing controller and crane are always labelled "UNCHANGED" or "Untouched — zero risk"
- A dashed feedback line runs from the crane back to craneIQ (load data, vibration, position)
