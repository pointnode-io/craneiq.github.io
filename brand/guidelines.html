06
Motion Principles
Animation in craneIQ interfaces should feel precise and considered — like instrumentation, not entertainment. Fast to respond, slow to settle. Never decorative for its own sake.
Easing
Use ease-out for elements entering the screen — fast start, gentle settle. Use ease-in-out for state transitions. Never linear or elastic easing in UI.
cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0.1, 1)
Duration
UI transitions: 120–180ms. Panel transitions: 240–320ms. Data readouts update immediately — no animation. Longer durations feel imprecise in industrial contexts.
120ms · 180ms · 280ms
Principles
Motion communicates state, not style. Status indicators pulse slowly and steadily. Urgency is communicated through colour change, not animation speed.
Purposeful · Precise · Calm
07
Usage Rules
✓ Always
  • Write craneIQ — lowercase c, uppercase IQ, no space or hyphen
  • Open Sans 300 for "crane", 600 for "IQ" — no other weights in the wordmark
  • Use teal #008B9A for "IQ" in the master mark
  • Use the product accent colour for "IQ" in product-specific contexts
  • Maintain 1× x-height clear space around the wordmark on all sides
  • Use Share Tech Mono for all serial numbers and specification data
  • Reference products in full: craneIQ Core, craneIQ Vision, craneIQ LoadTrack, etc.
  • Include CE and UKCA marks on all UK-sold hardware
  • Place craneIQ on the left in all co-branding arrangements
× Never
  • Write CraneIQ, CRANEIQ, Craneiq, crane IQ, or crane-IQ
  • Use Open Sans 700 or 900 — 600 SemiBold is the ceiling
  • Add a drop shadow, glow, outline, or text-shadow to the wordmark
  • Use a product accent in a context belonging to a different product
  • Use the brand teal as a background fill — it is a text and accent colour only
  • Render the wordmark below 14px digital or 4mm print
  • Use any font other than Open Sans and Share Tech Mono
  • Abbreviate to "IQ Core" or "the IQ" — always the full craneIQ prefix
