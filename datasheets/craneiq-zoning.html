Overview

Overhead crane collisions with buildings, equipment and other cranes are preventable — but they keep happening, because traditional safety measures rely entirely on the operator. craneIQ Zoning removes that reliance. Zone boundaries are configured in software — when a crane approaches a restricted area, the system automatically decelerates and stops it before it can enter. No barriers, no physical stops, no reliance on operator awareness. Whether you need to protect a structural column, keep cranes apart on a shared runway, or restrict access above a pedestrian area, craneIQ Zoning can be configured to suit your specific site layout.

What it does for you
  • Configurable no-fly zones — software definedDefine any restricted area in software — no physical barriers, no steelwork modifications
  • Multi-crane anti-collision on shared runwaysPrevents two cranes getting too close — automatically decelerates the approaching crane before contact
  • Protects building steelwork and structuresKeeps the crane away from columns, walls and roof elements that would be damaged by impact
  • Floor-level protection for machinery and personnelDefine zones above sensitive equipment, pedestrian walkways or areas where crane entry is not permitted
