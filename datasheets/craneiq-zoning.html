Overview

Overhead crane collisions with buildings, equipment and other cranes are preventable — but they keep happening because traditional safety measures rely entirely on the operator. craneIQ Zoning removes that reliance. Using laser distance sensors combined with software-defined zone boundaries, the system automatically decelerates and stops the crane before it enters a restricted area. Zones are configured in software — add a new machine to the shop floor, change a pedestrian route, or add a new restricted area, and the zone boundary updates in software without physically moving any sensors or hardware. As part of the craneIQ platform, Zoning integrates directly with craneIQ Core and craneIQ Cloud — zone events are logged, visible remotely, and part of the complete crane data picture.

Common scenarios
Crane to crane — shared runways
Two or more cranes sharing the same runway. craneIQ Zoning prevents them getting too close — automatically decelerating the approaching crane before contact can occur. No reliance on operators watching each other.
Crane to building — structural protection
Keep the crane away from columns, end walls, roof steelwork and other structural elements. Particularly important where cranes operate close to the building fabric or where visibility is limited.
No-fly zones — floor level machinery
Restrict crane entry above sensitive equipment, machine centres, or areas where a swinging load would cause damage. Zone boundaries can be defined at any height — crane level, load level, or both.
Personnel protection — walkways and offices
Prevent crane operation above pedestrian routes, welfare areas, or in-plant offices. A zone boundary above these areas ensures automatic intervention before the crane can enter — without relying on operator awareness.
