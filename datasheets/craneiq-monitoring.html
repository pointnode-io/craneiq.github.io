Overview

You would never run a fleet of vehicles with no odometers, no service indicators, and no way of knowing when something was wearing out. Yet most overhead cranes run exactly like that — no record of how many lifts they've made, no visibility of how much design life they've used, no early warning when something is changing. craneIQ Monitoring fixes that. It fits alongside any existing crane control system without touching it — no rewiring, no changes, zero risk to existing operations. From the moment it's switched on it starts recording: every lift logged, design life counted down from 100% based on actual usage, brake condition tracked, vibration trended. When something starts to change, you know about it before it becomes a breakdown. The data is yours — accessible on-site or via craneIQ Cloud — in open formats your engineers and third-party maintenance teams can work with directly.

Design Working Period (DWP)
DWP is the calculated remaining design life of your crane's critical components — hoist, brake, motor. It starts at 100% when new and counts down based on actual usage. craneIQ Monitoring tracks it continuously, so you know when components are approaching their service interval — before they fail.
Why it matters
Without DWP tracking, maintenance is either done too early — wasting money — or too late, risking unexpected failure. With craneIQ Monitoring, maintenance intervals are driven by actual crane usage rather than calendar dates or guesswork. Plan maintenance. Don't react to breakdowns.
